Metro Manila, 34 other areas seen to have ‘dangerous’ heat index

MANILA, Philippines — Metro Manila and 35 other areas in the country nationwide are expected to have dangerous levels of heat index on Monday, according to the state weather bureau.

The heat index under the “danger” category ranges from 42°C to 51°C, according to the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (Pagasa).

Pagasa said its station in Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Pasay and Science Garden in Quezon City are seen to record a heat index of 43°C and 42°C, respectively.
